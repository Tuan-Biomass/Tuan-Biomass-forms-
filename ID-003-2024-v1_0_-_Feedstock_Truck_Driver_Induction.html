Legal Obligations

Chain of Responsibility

Everyone in the transport supply chain shares responsibility for heavy vehicle safety and compliance with HVNL and CoR requirements.

  • 1Help with accurate load documentation and weight limits.
  • 2Follow safe loading and unloading procedures.
  • 3Report unsafe practices or CoR breaches immediately.
  • 4Vehicles must be roadworthy and maintained before entering site.
Fatigue Management

Fatigue Requirements

  • 1Comply with NHVR work and rest hour requirements.
  • 2Maintain an accurate and current log book.
  • 3Take required rest breaks and do not exceed legal driving limits.
  • 4Report fatigue concerns to site management.
  • 5If fatigued, stop work and seek help.
Personal Protective Equipment

PPE Requirements

The following PPE must be worn while on site. Failure to wear required PPE may result in removal from the site.

Day Shift
  • Steel-cap boots
  • Hard hat
  • High-visibility clothing
Night Shift
  • Steel-cap boots
  • Hard hat
  • High-visibility clothing with reflective tape
Drug & Alcohol Policy

Zero Tolerance

Drugs and alcohol are strictly prohibited on site. Workers must not be under the influence of alcohol or non-prescription drugs while working.

Warning: Any contractor attending site may be subject to random drug testing.
Feedstock Delivery

Traffic & Delivery Rules

To reduce collision risk between mobile plant and vehicles, traffic must flow one way around the site where possible unless special permission has been granted.

    Tip Over Axle Trucks

    Tipping Safety

    • 1The ring road must be clear when an axle tipper truck is unloading.
    • 2No vehicles or workers are allowed within 10 metres either side of the truck while tipping.
    • 3Tipping is not allowed in high winds or if the load is uneven.
    • 4Before raising the tipper, contact anyone in the vicinity and confirm the area is clear.
    • 5Confirm the ground is free of sawdust or chip. If the area is covered with debris, call the loader driver to scrape and clear the road before tipping.
    Emergency Procedures

    In an Emergency

    • 1Stop all work immediately.
    • 2Call for help using site communication channels or notify the nearest worker.
    • 3Dial 000 if emergency services are required.
    • 4Follow the evacuation plan and go to the nearest assembly point.
    • 5Do not return to work until the all-clear is given.
