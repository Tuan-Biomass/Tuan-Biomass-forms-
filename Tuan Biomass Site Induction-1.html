Welcome to Tuan Biomass

Tuan Biomass processes wood waste into fuel pellets for power stations. The site operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Wood fibre arrives either by conveyor from the Hyne sawmill or by truck through the front gate. The fibre is stored in silos, dried in the dehydration line, reduced in hammer mills, pressed into pellets, cooled, and then transferred into storage hoppers and bulk silos for truck loading and transport.

⚠ Safety is our core value. No production target, deadline or job urgency justifies unsafe work.
Every person on site — worker, contractor, or visitor — has the right and responsibility to stop work when conditions are unsafe.

How the Site Works — The Production Process

1
Fibre In
Wood waste arrives by conveyor from Hyne Sawmill or by truck through the front gate.
2
Fibre Silos
Raw fibre is stored in silos before drying.
3
Dehydration Line
Wet fibre passes through the dryer drum at high heat to reduce moisture.
4
Hammer Mills
Dried fibre is ground down into fine particles.
5
Pellet Presses
Fine fibre is pressed under high pressure into dense fuel pellets.
6
Cooler & Storage
Hot pellets are cooled, then transferred to hoppers and bulk silos.
7
Truck Loading
Pellets are loaded from silos into trucks for transport to the port of Bundaberg.

Hazard 01 & 02 — Combustible Dust, Fire & Spontaneous Combustion

01 — COMBUSTIBLE DUST & EXPLOSION
Wood dust suspended in air can EXPLODE when ignited.

High-risk areas:
  • Hammer mills
  • Pellet presses
  • Conveyors & cyclones
  • Storage silos

Controls:
  • Dust extraction systems
  • No ignition sources (no open flames, no unapproved electrical equipment)
  • Strict housekeeping — report any dust build-up immediately
02 — FIRE & SPONTANEOUS COMBUSTION
Biomass fibre and pellets can self-heat and ignite.

High-risk areas:
  • Dehydration line & dryer drum
  • Fibre silos & pellet coolers
  • Conveyors

Causes:
  • Hot bearings, friction heat, dryer faults
  • Loss of control of process
  • Electrical failure, self-heating fibre

Controls:
  • Grecon fire detection & suppression system
  • Temperature monitoring
  • Immediate reporting of unusual heat, smoke or odour

Hazard 03 & 04 — Stored Energy & Mobile Plant

Forklift — 6m exclusion zone applies
Wheel loader — mobile plant has right of way
03 — STORED ENERGY (LOTO REQUIRED)
Uncontrolled release of stored energy causes serious injury or death.

Energy types on site:
  • Electrical
  • Hydraulic pressure
  • Pneumatic pressure
  • Mechanical / spring-loaded
  • Thermal heat

RULE: Equipment MUST be fully isolated, locked, tagged and tested DEAD before any maintenance. Never work on running equipment.
04 — MOBILE PLANT & TRAFFIC
Collisions with mobile equipment are fatal.

Equipment operating on site:
  • Front wheel loaders
  • Forklifts
  • Telehandlers
  • Trucks & delivery vehicles

Rules:
  • Pedestrians stay on marked walkways
  • Radio on UHF Ch 34 before crossing ring road
  • Never walk behind reversing plant
  • Exclusion zone of 6m around mobile plant
  • Mobile plant has right of way

Hazard 05 & 06 — Working at Heights & Confined Spaces

Heights work on site — harness & permit required
Caged ladder access — trained personnel only
05 — WORKING AT HEIGHTS
Falls from height are a leading cause of workplace fatalities.

Height work occurs during:
  • Silo & conveyor maintenance
  • Roof work
  • Elevated structure access

Rules:
  • Collective protection first (guardrails, platforms)
  • Harness required when risk assessment confirms PPE needed
  • Complete Harness Inspection Checklist (HIC-001-2025-v1.0) before use
  • Exclusion zone below all overhead work
  • Only trained personnel
06 — CONFINED SPACES
Confined spaces can cause death by engulfment, oxygen deficiency or toxic atmosphere.

Confined spaces include:
  • Fibre & pellet silos
  • Cyclones & ducting
  • Process vessels

Rules:
  • NEVER enter a confined space without a valid permit (CSP-001-2024-v1.0)
  • Atmosphere must be tested before entry
  • A trained attendant must remain outside at all times
  • Written emergency rescue plan required
  • Only trained personnel
Albioma Tuan Biomass  |  ID-003-2025-v1.0

Hazard 07 & 08 — Engulfment & Hot Work

Hot work — permit required, 2hr fire watch after completion
07 — ENGULFMENT & MATERIAL COLLAPSE
Fibre and pellets in silos and hoppers can collapse without warning.

Risks:
  • Bridging — fibre forms a crust over a void
  • Wall collapse when material shifts
  • Rapid engulfment — you can sink in seconds

Rules:
  • NEVER enter a silo or hopper without confined space controls
  • Stay 25m clear of working stacks
  • Stay 5m clear of formed stacks
  • No walking on stored material surfaces
08 — HOT WORK
Grinding, welding & cutting create sparks and ignition sources in a combustible dust environment.

Requirements before starting:
  • Hot Work Permit (HWP-001-2024-v1.0)
  • 5D area inspection (above, below, front, behind, inside)
  • Remove or shield combustible material
  • Fire extinguisher immediately available

During work:
  • Monitor sparks & impact points
  • Fire watch: minimum 2 hours after work is complete
  • Fire detection systems only disabled by authorised person

Training Module A — Isolation & Lockout / Tagout (LOTO)

WHY LOTO MATTERS: Uncontrolled stored energy kills. Equipment that appears off can still be energised. LOTO ensures no one can start equipment while another worker is exposed.
STEP 1 — PREPARE
Identify the job and all energy sources.
STEP 2 — ISOLATE
Isolate all energy sources: electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, thermal and mechanical.
STEP 3 — RELEASE
Release, vent or bleed stored/residual energy. Block or secure anything that could move.
STEP 4 — LOCK & TAG
Apply your personal lockout device to the isolation point. Attach a tag with your name.
STEP 5 — VERIFY
Test that the equipment is DEAD before starting work.
STEP 6 — COMPLETE
Tags stay on until ALL work is done. Only the person who applied the lock may remove it.

More complex isolations require a Lockout Officer and the completion of an Isolation Request Form.

Albioma Tuan Biomass  |  ID-003-2025-v1.0

LOTO — Critical Rules You Must Know

🔴Only lock out tags must be worn on your person at all times.
🔒Every worker involved in the job must apply their own personal lockout device — one lock per person.
🏷️Lockout tags must show the worker's name and remain in place until ALL work is complete.
⚡Absence of energy must be verified by testing — assume it is live until proven dead.
🚫Never remove another person's lock. If you find a damaged or unexplained lock, stop and contact the Safety Manager.
📋The LOTO form (LOP-001-2024-v1.0) must be completed and approved before complex isolation begins.
📚Required training: TM-001 (Site Isolation), SWI-003 (Lockout Officers), SWI-004 (LOTO)

Training Module B — Manual Handling

Manual handling injuries are one of the most common workplace injuries. Poor technique causes long-term damage to backs, shoulders, and knees.

✅ CORRECT TECHNIQUE
  • Plan the lift — clear your path first
  • Stand close to the load
  • Feet shoulder-width apart for stability
  • Bend at the knees — keep back straight
  • Grip firmly before lifting
  • Lift with leg muscles, not your back
  • Keep load close to your body
  • Turn by moving your feet, never twist
  • Lower with control — bend knees again
❌ AVOID THESE ACTIONS
  • Twisting at the waist while lifting
  • Bending your back instead of your knees
  • Reaching too far from your body
  • Lifting above shoulder height alone
  • Carrying a load that blocks your view
  • Rushing — plan the lift first
  • Ignoring pain — report any discomfort immediately
Albioma Tuan Biomass  |  ID-003-2025-v1.0

Manual Handling — Risk Controls & Requirements

🔍 Assess before lifting
Use a Take 5 or JSA for any manual task involving awkward postures, heavy loads, or repetition. Plan the lift before you start.
🤝 Team lifts
Loads over 25 kg should be lifted by two or more people. Never attempt a heavy lift alone if team assistance is available.
🦾 Use mechanical aids
Forklifts, trolleys, pallet jacks and hoists must be used wherever practical to eliminate manual lifting.
📋 Training required
All workers must complete TM-002 (Manual Handling) and TLID0020 (Shift Materials Safely) before performing manual handling tasks.
🏥 Report injuries immediately
Report any pain, strain or discomfort immediately to the supervisor. Early reporting leads to faster recovery and prevents worsening.
⚠️ Ergonomic hazards
Tuan Biomass specific risks include handling die segments, silo access equipment, conveyor parts, and bag goods. All assessed in TM-002.

Reporting Hazards, Near Misses & Incidents

If you see it — report it. Early reporting prevents incidents from becoming fatalities.
HAZARD
Any condition or situation with the potential to cause harm, damage or loss.
→ Report to Plant Manager or Safety Manager immediately. Photograph if safe. Apply immediate controls where possible.
NEAR MISS
An unplanned event that did not result in injury but had the potential to.
→ Report immediately to supervisor. Lessons must be communicated to the team as soon as possible.
INJURY
Any work-related event resulting in physical harm or requiring first aid or medical treatment.
→ Report to Plant Manager or Safety Manager. First aid treatment and recording in the First Aid Treatment Record Book is mandatory.
HIPO EVENT
Any event that COULD HAVE caused serious injury, fatality or major plant damage.
→ Immediate notification to Plant Manager & Safety Manager. Secure the area. Formal investigation commences as priority.

Fit for Work — Drug, Alcohol & Fatigue Policy

Zero BAC at all times while on site or on duty. No exceptions.
Alcohol
  • Zero Blood Alcohol Concentration (BAC) at all times on site
  • No alcohol in the 8 hours prior to a shift
  • Random testing may be conducted
  • Testing may be required after an incident
Drugs
  • No recreational drugs during the roster period that may impair work
  • Prescription medication that affects alertness must be reported to the Plant Manager — confidential
  • Random testing applies
Fatigue
  • Minimum 10 hours rest between shifts
  • Standard roster: 7 days on / 7 days off
  • Warning signs: micro-sleeping, poor coordination, aggression, ignoring alarms
  • Do not perform LOTO, confined space, hot work or heights when fatigued
  • Report fatigue to supervisor immediately

Non-Compliance — Consequences & Your Rights

Consequences of Non-Compliance
  • Immediate work stoppage
  • Requirement to provide corrective actions before work resumes
  • Serious non-compliance: root cause analysis + improvement plan required
  • Removal from site for serious or repeated violations
Your Rights as a Worker
  • Right to stop unsafe work at any time — no repercussions
  • Right to be consulted on safety decisions that affect your work
  • Right to report hazards and incidents without fear
  • Right to refuse work that is unsafe and unclear
  • Right to access PPE, procedures and safety documents
  • Right to rehabilitation and support if injured at work
