Legal Obligations

Chain of Responsibility (CoR)

Under CoR laws, everyone in the transport supply chain shares responsibility for ensuring heavy vehicle safety and compliance with the Heavy Vehicle National Law (HVNL).

  • 1Ensure accurate load documentation and adherence to weight limits to prevent overloading.
  • 2Follow safe loading and unloading procedures to minimise risks.
  • 3Report any unsafe practices or breaches of CoR obligations immediately.
  • 4Vehicles must be roadworthy and properly maintained before entering the site.
Fatigue Management

Fatigue Requirements

  • 1Comply with work and rest hour regulations as per the NHVR.
  • 2Maintain an accurate and up-to-date log book.
  • 3Take mandatory rest breaks and not exceed legal driving limits.
  • 4Report any fatigue-related concerns to site management immediately.
  • 5If feeling fatigued, stop work and seek assistance — do not continue operating a vehicle unsafely.

Drug & Alcohol Policy

Zero Tolerance

The use of drugs and alcohol is strictly prohibited while on site.

Warning: Any worker suspected of being under the influence may be removed from site.

      HazardControls
      Emergency Procedures

      In Case of an Emergency

      • 1Stop all work immediately.
      • 2Call for help using site communication channels or notify the nearest worker.
      • 3Follow the emergency evacuation plan and proceed to the nearest assembly point.
      • 4Do not return to work until the all-clear is given by the site supervisor.
      Fire or Hazardous Incident: Report smoke, fire, or a hazardous spill immediately to the site office or on-shift operator. Evacuate to the designated assembly point.
      Medical Emergency: Dial 000. Do not move an injured person unless they are in immediate danger.
